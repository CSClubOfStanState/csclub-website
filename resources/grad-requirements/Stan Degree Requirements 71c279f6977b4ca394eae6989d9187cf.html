Academic Path

Students need to picture their path through their 4-years at Stan State. As part of this path, students can track their degree progress to ensure they stay on track to graduate in 4 years. Stan State offers some great tools and resources for students to take advantage of.

myStanState Portal and MyStanState Student Center

Inside the myStanState Portal, students can access their MyStanState Student Center, in which, after logging in, they can see a list of useful tools. STAN Degree Progress is a degree audit report tool that students can use to further their progress toward completing degree requirements.

  1. Search - Allows students to quickly look for courses offered in past and upcoming terms if applicable.
  1. Enroll - Students can view their current class schedule and even add, drop, or swap courses. Edit allows students to change their course grading options from Letter Grade/Credit No Credit. Term Information displays relevant dates of the academic semester.
  1. My Academics - Quick view academic requirements, what-if reports, transfer credit, etc.
  1. STAN Planner - Stan State Generates a plan for students to work on their degree progress. (Not Recommended)
  1. STAN Scheduler - Students SHOULD use this tool to generate schedules based on their degree plans and availability before registering for courses.

What If this and that? + Catalog

What if there is a class I can use to double dip for this and that? TRACK it! You can take many courses to help you get an edge on your academic career. Please take advantage of these courses by learning about them from your advisors/faculty, and make sure that others know about them too!

Typically, the year you enroll at Stan State designates your catalog year for your Stan Degree Progress. Your year’s catalog will NOT change during the duration of your academic enrollment unless a change is enacted from above. For example, the WPST was an exam required for the 2018 catalog students to complete before attempting to take any Upper Division courses and their Writing Proficiency courses. As of March 12, 2020, the WPST was removed as a requirement per the CSU’s Chancellor’s Office in a memorandum.

This requirement was FULLY removed from Stan Degree Progress.

Computer Science 2018 Catalog + Tips & Tricks

Even though some of these tips are from the 2018 catalog, they may apply to you.

  1. NSCI 1000: Information Investigation in STEM Discipline - This course can fulfill your requirements for Area E: Lifelong Learning and Self Development.
  1. CS3500: Human-Centered Design - This course fulfills requirements for Area B: Natural Sci and Math/Quantitative Reasoning: UD B GE Area AND counts as a Major Requirements, Requirement 4 for Electives. Note that taking this class invalidates your ability to take courses: CS 3550 and CS 4010 for elective credit.
  1. HIST 2600: Problems in U.S. History - This course can ONLY be taken as a first- or second-year student (based on your completed units), so do not miss out on this course early on. If you do, taking an equivalent course at a Junior College or an upper division equivalent course at Stan State is recommended.
  1. KINS4330: Family Health - This course fulfills your GE Multicultural Requirement and your Area UD-D Upper Division D requirements.
